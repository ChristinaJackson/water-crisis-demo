Water Crisis Project

We have not forgotten about Flint.

The Problem

Tens of thousands of Flint residents still do not have clean water and aid is inconsistent. Some people don't qualify for help and others must travel out of the city, regardless of transportation restrictions, to get bottled water. Some wait hours in line just to be turned away.

25% or more of the water used to fill water bottles could come from the tap. However, tap water can contain contaminants like heavy metals, arsenic, and BPAs.

Often, plastic bottles are not recycled and end up in our oceans and water sources.

Our Mission

The Water Crisis Project puts the power back into people's hands by teaching them how to purify their own water and provides them with the resources necessary to do so. We supply countertop distillers for personal, in-home use. This provides people with the convenience of having clean water in their own home, especially for those who cannot travel.

Distillers remove heavy metals like lead and copper, creating 4 gallons of pure, clean drinking water per day.
